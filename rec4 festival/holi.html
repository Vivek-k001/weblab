Holi - Festival of colours

Holi, known as the Festival of Colors, is celebrated across India in spring. People play with colored powders and water, sing and dance, and enjoy festive sweets like gujiya. Holi symbolizes the victory of good over evil and the arrival of spring. The festival usually begins with Holika Dahan, a bonfire on the night before Holi, representing the burning away of evil. On the main day, friends and families gather outdoors, smearing each other with vibrant colors and exchanging greetings. Traditional music, folk songs, and lively processions fill the streets, creating a joyful and inclusive atmosphere. Holi also promotes forgiveness, unity, and the renewal of relationships, as people let go of past grievances and celebrate together.
